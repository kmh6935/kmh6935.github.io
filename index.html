About me

Minhyeok Ko received his B.S. and M.E. in Architectural Engineering from Chung-Ang University, South Korea in 2012 and 2014, respectively, his M.S. in Civil Engineering (Structural Engineering) from Northwestern University, USA in 2016, and his Ph.D. in Civil Engineering (Structural Engineering) from The Pennsylvania State University, USA in 2023. After working as a Postdoctoral Research Associate in the Department of Civil, Environmental and Geodetic Engineering at The Ohio State University (2023–2025), he joined the faculty of the Department of Civil and Construction Engineering and Management at The University of Texas at Tyler, USA, to serve as an Assistant Professor (2025–present).

Dr. Ko teaches undergraduate and graduate courses in structural engineering and materials, including mechanics of materials, civil engineering materials, and related subjects. His research interests include (1) digital twin technology for civil infrastructure, (2) structural health monitoring and system identification, (3) uncertainty quantification and risk-informed decision-making, (4) physics-informed and data-driven modeling, and (5) resilience assessment of infrastructure systems under natural hazards. Dr. Ko has authored and co-authored publications in peer-reviewed archival journals and presented his work at national and international conferences, advancing the integration of physics-based modeling and artificial intelligence for real-time monitoring, predictive modeling, and decision-making in complex structural systems.

Dr. Ko is a member of the American Society of Civil Engineers (ASCE) and other professional organizations. He has been involved in collaborative research initiatives bridging structural engineering, computational mechanics, and infrastructure resilience, and has contributed as a reviewer for several leading international journals in structural engineering and computational modeling.
